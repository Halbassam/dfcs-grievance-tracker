Help & User Guide

How to use the FCRC Grievance Tracker

Quick start

This app tracks grievances and pre-grievance investigations for your AFSCME local. Here is the typical workflow:

  1. File a grievance — go to Intake Form, fill in the employee details, pick the CBA article and grievance type, and save. The app calculates contractual deadlines automatically.
  2. Track it — the Dashboard shows all active cases ranked by next deadline, flags overdue ones in red, and shows the Steward Workload summary.
  3. Log activity — inside any grievance detail view, use the Activity Log to record meetings, phone calls, and next steps.
  4. Advance through steps — fill in the Step 1, 2, and 3 date fields as the grievance progresses. The app updates deadlines automatically.
  5. Archive resolved cases — once a case is settled, withdrawn, or denied, use the Archive button to move it out of the active list.

Account roles

Every login belongs to one of three roles. Admins assign roles in Settings → Manage users.

Steward
Files and tracks grievances at their assigned location(s). Cannot access the AI drafting bot, Manage Users, or Settings.
Steward Plus
Everything a Steward can do, plus access to the Draft a Grievance AI bot and the Investigations workflow.
Admin
Full access — all features, all locations, plus Settings (manage users, locations, setup lists, org info, email configuration).

ⓘ Location assignment is separate from role. A "super steward" covering multiple offices is just a steward (or steward plus) with two or more locations checked in Manage Users.

Intake Form — filing a grievance

Grievance ID — enter your local's standard ID format (e.g. G-2026-047). Must be unique — the system rejects duplicates.

Awareness date — the date the steward (or grievant) first became aware of the violation. This is the clock-start for contractual filing deadlines under Article V.

CBA article / Section picker — select the article first, then the section picker below it will show only that article's numbered sections. You can still type freely in the section field if the exact subsection isn't listed.

Location — single-location stewards see this pre-filled and locked. Admins and stewards covering multiple offices see a dropdown.

Deadlines panel — appears automatically once you enter the Awareness date. The app calculates Step 1, 2, and 3 filing and response deadlines per Article V, skipping holidays you have configured. Always verify deadlines against the actual contract before relying on them.

Saving — click Save grievance to create or update the record. The system confirms with a toast notification. To edit an existing grievance, click its ID anywhere in the Dashboard or log to load it back into the form.

Investigations — pre-grievance fact-finding

Not every complaint becomes a formal grievance. The Investigations workflow lets you fact-find first — gather statements, log observations, and decide whether a violation actually occurred — before committing to a formal filing.

Creating an investigation — click + New investigation, enter the employee name, optional email, steward, and location. The system assigns a sequential ID (INV-YYYY-NNNN).

Grievant intake form — click Generate & copy grievant link to get a unique, 7-day link you can text or email to the grievant. They fill out a short statement form on their phone or computer — no login required. Their submission appears in the investigation automatically.

Steward logbook — use the logbook to record your own notes, interviews, observations, and next steps as the investigation progresses. Entries are timestamped and can't be deleted.

Closing an investigation — if you determine no formal grievance is needed, click Close case and select a reason: No Violation, Resolved, Withdrew, or Other.

Converting to a grievance — if the facts support filing, click Convert ➜ Grievance, enter a Grievance ID, and the Intake Form pre-fills automatically with the employee's details and statement.

Archiving — closed or converted investigations can be archived to keep the list clean. Click Archive in the detail panel. Use Show archived at the top to view them later.

Draft a Grievance — AI assistant (Steward Plus and Admin only)

The AI drafting bot helps stewards write Statements of Grievance grounded in the actual AFSCME Master Contract (2023–2027). It searches relevant Article and Section text, asks clarifying questions if facts are missing, and produces a full draft with numbered Relief Sought.

How to use it — describe the situation in plain language. The bot will ask follow-up questions (dates, what was said/done, whether progressive discipline was followed, etc.) and then draft the grievance once it has enough facts.

Copying the draft — click Copy as document to copy a formatted Statement of Grievance to your clipboard, ready to paste into Word or an email. Click Use this draft in the Intake Form to pre-fill the formal grievance form automatically.

Inside investigations — the bot is also available inside an open investigation. When used there, it automatically receives the employee's submitted statement, your logbook entries, and other investigation facts as context before you even type a word.

⚠ Important limitations:
  • The bot uses the contract text for citations but is not hallucination-proof. Always verify the Article/Section it cites against the actual printed contract before filing.
  • The verbatim "Contract Language" quote shown in the draft is the bot's best attempt at copying the real text — check it against the actual contract page.
  • The bot does not know your local's history with a supervisor, unwritten past-practice arguments, or political factors. A steward's judgment on strategy still applies.
  • For anything high-stakes (discipline up to discharge, group grievances, likely arbitration) — consult the local grievance chair or Council 31 staff before filing, regardless of what the draft says.

Dashboard

Priority Case Monitor — active grievances sorted by next contractual deadline. Red rows are overdue. Cases past Step 3 are not shown here — Council 31 tracks Step 4 and arbitration separately.

Steward Workload — one card per steward showing their active caseload by step. A red border means they have at least one overdue deadline. Admins see a location filter dropdown in the header to narrow the view by office.

Location filter (admin only) — use the dropdown in the top-right of the Priority Monitor to filter the entire dashboard view to one office. Stewards always see only their own location(s) and can't switch.

Settings (Admin only)

Local chapter info — stores the org-wide values (Agency, Local No., Bureau, County, Shift) that auto-fill on every printed grievance. Select a location in the dropdown to load and save that office's bureau and management email.

Location contact details — configure a bureau name and management email address for each of your offices in one grid. The management email is used for courtesy reminder notices — each location gets its own address.

Manage users — create, edit, and delete login accounts. Assign a role (Steward / Steward Plus / Admin) and one or more locations per account. A steward with no location assigned cannot view or file grievances until one is set.

Edit lists — configure the dropdown options shown on the Intake Form: CBA articles, grievance types, job classifications, bargaining units, locations, shifts, and activity types. The Article and Grievance Type lists were pre-seeded from the 2023–2027 contract — edit them to match how your local actually categorizes cases.

Location assignment check — shows any grievances or steward accounts missing a location. Useful after first deployment or when onboarding a new office.

Holiday list — enter observed holidays so the deadline calculator skips them correctly. Holidays are used by the Article V deadline math — missing a holiday here could cause deadlines to be off by a day.

Tips & common questions

The Dashboard shows a different list than I expect — the list is filtered to your assigned location(s). If you believe a case is missing, check with an admin to confirm its location field is set correctly.

The grievance ID already exists — IDs must be unique. If you're updating an existing case, the system detects the ID and updates it in-place rather than creating a duplicate. If you genuinely want a new case, use a new ID.

The deadline calculator shows N/A — the Awareness date is required to calculate any deadlines. Set it first, then the Step 1 filing deadline will appear.

Emails are not arriving — go to Settings → Email status to check whether BREVO_API_KEY and BREVO_SENDER_EMAIL are configured. Also check the Email Log for recent send attempts and any error messages. The steward's email address must be entered in Settings → Manage stewards for them to receive deadline reminders.

The AI bot cited the wrong Article — always verify citations against the printed contract. The bot's verbatim quote is the key check: if the quote doesn't appear in the contract, the citation is wrong. Start a fresh conversation and describe the situation with more specific detail if the first attempt missed the relevant article.

I want to see archived grievances — go to the Archive tab. Archived cases are read-only. If a case needs to be re-opened (e.g. a denial was appealed), contact an admin — there is no un-archive button by design, since re-opening requires a judgment call.
